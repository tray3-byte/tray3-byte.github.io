About Me

I help organizations tell their stories

I've spent most of my career working with a variety of print and digital content, writing copy and shaping strategies that help organizations show up across the channels they want and connecting where it matters most. I've spent most of that time has been spent at Tanner Health, a regional healthcare system in west Georgia, where I grew from an intern to a content strategist creating content for the regional health system's internal and external marketing and communications campaigns.

In content development, much of my focus is in strategy and execution, where I don't just plan content — I write it, launch it, manage and measure it as I push it out into the world. Whether it's a press release, a social media campaign, a website or an internal communication, wrap my strategy around who's reading, how they're engaging, why they should care and what they should do next.

In addition to managing content, I've been fortunate to curate an eclectic set of skills that have allowed me to lend support across graphic and web design, photography and videography, search engine optimization, campaign analytics and even computer science and IT — two industries I'm particularly passionate about — so much that I've studied the field and picked up a couple of COMPTIA certifications, which taught me not only about computer systems and configuration, but also how the best communicators understand the systems where their content lives.

Surgery Without Borders

Dr. José E. Espinel, MD, a surgeon specializing in minimally-invasive and robotic surgery, became among the first physicians to deploy the da Vinci 5 robotic surgical platform to broadcast a live procedure to his medical colleagues in Ecuador. To record the moment and his achievement, I photographed him as he performed the procedure and wrote a short post to share on the health system's social media channels, capturing both his clinical milestone and the broader story of how a regional community hospital in west Georgia can deliver world-class care.

Built to Heal

When Tanner Medical Center/Carrollton opened its new patient tower in 2025, the story wasn't just about the expansion or its square footage. It was about what the new facility meant to the community it serves and how its architecture was designed to help heal those receiving care inside it. As part of my year-long coverage of the expansion project, I captured photos and wrote a feature covering the istallation of a nearly 7,500-pound wind sculpture created by local artists for the tower's courtyard.

Crisis Communications

Crisis Communications

From weather disasters to active shooters, when an emergency strikes or the unthinkable happens, hospitals have to be prepared to respond. To do so, Tanner Health hospitals and patient care teams conduct drills alongside law enforcement, fire departments and emergency medical services to practice how they will operate, communicate and coordinate. During these crisis trainings, I served as a public information officer responsible for coordinating the communications plan in a real-world situation. While these are only drills, I also coverd the story of how even the best communities have to be prepared for the worst events to ensure the safety of its residents.
